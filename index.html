If the Earth is a chicken, I hope it is a homogeneous and isotropic spherical chicken in a vacuum.

Welcome! I am Linxuan Li (李林轩), an undergraduate majoring in Geophysics at Wuhan University. I will begin my PhD life in Seismo Lab

at California Institute of Technology. I am keen on everything associated with tectonics, especially earthquakes. My research focuses on both observational data and theoretical models. I use techniques from disciplines such as seismology, geodynamics, geodesy, math, and physics.

During my stay at Wuhan University, I mainly focus on the response of Earth's crust to various processes and the magnitude-frequency distributions of earthquake sequences. I hope to devote my life to exploring the phenomena associated with and the physical processes behind earthquakes/volcanoes/landslides/tsunamis so that we can better understand our planet, produce clean energy, and mitigate natural disasters.
