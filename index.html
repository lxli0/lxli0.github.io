Welcome! I am Linxuan Li, an undergraduate majoring in Geophysics at Wuhan University. I am keen on everything associated with tectonics, especially earthquakes. My research focuses on both observational data and theatrical models. I use techniques from disciplines such as seismology, geodynamics, geodesy, math, and physics.

During my stay at Wuhan University, I mainly focus on how the earth's crust responds to surface hydrosphere variations. I conduct several studies from different perspectives, such as surface deformation, seismicity rate, and seismic velocity. I find it is an interdisciplinary problem and can provide ideal natural experiments to help understand the crust and earthquakes.

In the future, I will explore broader areas. I hope to devote my life to exploring the phenomena associated with and the physical processes behind earthquakes so that we can better understand our planet and may survive natural disasters.
